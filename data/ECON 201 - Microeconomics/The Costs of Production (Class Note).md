## Glossary

- **Total revenue**: the amount a firm receives for the sale of its output
- **Total cost**: the market value of the inputs a firm uses in production
- **Profit**: total revenue minus total cost
- **Opportunity cost**: the cost of something is what you give up to get it
- **Outlay**: The spending or disbursement of money
- **Diminishing marginal production**: the property whereby the marginal product of an input declines as the quantity of the input increases
- **Efficient scale**: the lowest point where the plant (or firm) can produce such that its long run average costs are minimized with production remaining effective

---

Explicit costs require an outlay of money while implicit costs do not require a cash outlay.

`Total cost = explicit + implicit` or `TC (total cost) = FC (fixed cost) + VC (variable cost)`

It can be viewed that in the short run, there is a fixed cost, but there is no fixed cost in the long run as it is more flexible in the long run.

Adding more labor in order to increase the output might lead to a diminishing return, for instance adding more workers make the workspace more crowded - reducing the productivity of each worker. This makes the average total cost higher as the firm will have to pay a lot more for just one more quantity of product. Thus, it might be worth investing into increasing the workspace to compensate and eliminate the reduction in productivity, therefore the firm doesn't have to hire as many workers to produce more products; meaning the average total costs goes down.

**Economies of scale** refer to the cost advantages a company gains with an increase in production. This occurs because production costs can now be spread over a larger number of goods. The bigger the size of a company, the bigger the cost savings with the increase in production.
