## Glossary

- **Deadweight loss**: the loss of societal economic welfare due to production/consumption of a good at a quantity where marginal benefit (to society) does not equal marginal cost (to society). The fall in taxpayers' well-being exceeds the revenue the government collects
- **VAT**: value added taxes
- **Tariff**: tax imposed by the government of a country or by a supranational union on imports or exports of goods
- **Average tax rate**: total taxes paid divided by total income
- **Marginal tax rate**: the amount by which taxes increase from an additional dollar of income
- **Lump-sum tax**: a tax that is the same amount for every person
- **Benefits principle**: the idea that people should pay taxes based the benefits they receive from government services

---

The burden of the tax is shared between buyers and sellers depending on the price elasticities of demand and supply.

Personal income tax: the family's tax liability (how much it owes) is based on its total income.

A payroll tax (social insurance taxes) is a tax on the wages that a firm pays its workers.

Cooperate income tax: taxes each corporation based on its profit.

Other taxes include excise taxes, which are taxes on specific goods like gasoline, cigarettes, and alcoholic beverages. It also includes various small items, such as estate taxes and customs duties.

An efficient tax system is one that imposes small deadweight losses and small administrative burdens.

The total surplus when there is a tax imposed: `TS = PS + CS + tax`.
