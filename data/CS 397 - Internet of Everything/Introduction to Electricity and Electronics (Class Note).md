# Course Introduction

Telecommunication refers to the act of relaying and receiving information through electronic means, regardless of distance.

Criteria for wireless telecommunication includes frequency .

> Frequency $\neq$ Bandwidth

> For AC electricity, there are specifications that say 220V (which is the like the amplitude in the wave graph) at 60Hz (which is the frequency) for example. In AC electricity, the wave does not go below 0, instead it bounces back up. But, there are still times when the amplitude drops rapidly to 0V and back to the 220V maximum. Thus having a high frequency such as at 60Hz (60 circles per period) reduces the amount of time at which the electricity sits at 0V and increases the amount of time at which the electricity sit at the maximum amplitude, thus improving stability.

> The difference being subscribed to different telecommunication companies is the channel you use. Channels are discrete because they operate on different frequency (think of FM radio channels).

# Introduction to Electricity and Electronics

Current is the flow of electron. The conductor (such as conductor wire e.g. copper wire), which produces conductivity ($\sigma$), allows particles to flow.

Electrons must come free off atoms to generate current flow.

$I = \frac{V}{R}$: current ($A$)

> $V$: voltage ($V$) \
> $R$: resistance ($\ohm$)

> Good conductor has low resistivity

$R = \rho \frac{l}{A}$: resistance ($\ohm$)

> $\rho$: material (of the wire) resistivity ($\ohm m$) \
> $l$: length of wire ($m$) \
> $A$: cross-section area of wire ($m^2$)

> ESD: electrostatic discharge

> HV Tx Line = High Voltage Transmission Line

The resistor's function is to limit the current flow. This can be used to tailor the voltage from the source to fit the requirement of the components.

> E-fields (electric fields) are important for modern communication.

## Current Flow Theories

> A 5V battery can mean the positive pole has 5V and negative pole has 0V. The battery is 5V is because the +5V from the positive pole and 0V from the negative pole produce the potential difference $+5 - 0 = 5V$

The conventional flow of current is from (+) to (-). Another theory states that the flow follows the flow of electron which is (-) to (+) because all the electrons are stored in the negative pole. But the direction of the current flow does not make a difference to the three measurable units of electricity: voltage, current, and resistance.

The placement of resistors in a circuit does not change the current, i.e. putting a resistor before an appliance or after does not make a difference in performance. This is because a circuit is a closed loop.
