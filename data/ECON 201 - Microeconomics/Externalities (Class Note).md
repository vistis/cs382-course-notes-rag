## Glossary

- **Efficient**: being able to make the most out of something or achieve something with the minimal input
- **Market**: A market in economics refers to the whole area of operation of demand and supply, where buyers and sellers interact with each other to exchange goods and services. It is not limited to a fixed location, but rather encompasses the entire region where transactions take place. The market is characterized by the forces of supply and demand, which determine the prices of goods and services
- **Externality**: the uncompensated impact of one person's actions on the well-being of a bystander
- **Market failure**: a situation defined by an inefficient distribution of goods and services in the free market
- **Subsidy**: a type of government expenditure for individuals and households, as well as businesses with the aim of stabilizing the economy
- **Tax**: a compulsory financial charge or some other type of levy imposed on a taxpayer (an individual or legal entity) by a governmental organization in order to collectively fund government spending, public expenditures, or as a way to regulate and reduce negative externalities
- **Internalizing the externality**: altering incentives so that people take into account the external effects of their actions

---

> Markets are usually a good way to organize economy activity

Welfare refers the the well-being of market participants.

Consumer Surplus is the benefit the consumers gain by participating in the market. While Producer Surplus is the benefit that producers get from participating in the market.

# Negative Externalities

`TS = CS + PS - SC`, where

- `TS` is the total surplus
- `CS` is the consumer surplus
- `PS` is the producer surplus
- `SC` is the social cost or EC (external cost). SC is negative if the externality is a negative one, and is positive if it is a positive externality

When the producers take the social cost into consideration, the new "equilibrium" is now called "social optimum".

# Positive Externalitiess

Positive externality affects the demand curve. Negative externality affects the supply curve.

`TS = CS + PS + SV`, where

- `TS` is the total surplus
- `CS` is the consumer surplus
- `PS` is the producer surplus
- `SV` is the social value (social benefit)
