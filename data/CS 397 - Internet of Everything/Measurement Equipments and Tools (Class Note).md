## Electronic Components

The I-V relation in passive components is linear, and it is non-linear for active components.

An example of an active component is  a transistor which is used as a switch and an amplifier. Another is diode.

$C = \epsilon \frac{A}{d}$ where $\epsilon$ is permittivity, $A$ is cross-section area of the wire, and $d$ is the diameter of the wire cross-section. It is related to electricity.

$L = \mu \frac{N^2A}{l}$ where $\mu$ is the permeability, is related to magnetism.

A capacitor can store excess power, and can serve as a short-term backup voltage source.

$T$, period, is time to complete a circle in a wave (or the wave pattern repeats). $f$, frequency, is how many complete circles that can be made during one second.
