## Glossary

- **Excludability**: property of a good whereby a person can be prevented from using it
- **Rivalry in consumption**: each person's use reduces others' ability to use
- **Private goods**: goods that are both excludable and rival in consumption
- **Public goods**: goods that are neither excludable nor rival in consumption
- **Common resources**: goods that are rival in consumption but not excludable
- **Club goods**: goods that are excludable but not rival in consumption

---

> Governments can sometimes improve market outcomes

||Excludable|Not excludable|
|-|-|-|
|Rivalry in consumption|With toll and congested (private good)|No toll and congested (common resource)|
|No rivalry in consumption|With toll and not congested (club good)|No toll and not congested (public good)|

The impact of public good is the same as positive externality and the impact of common resource is the same as negative externality.
